Course Structure

The B.E. Computer System Engineering program consists of 8 semesters, with a total of 136 credit hours. The program includes:
Core Courses: Computer Programming, Data Structures, Algorithms, Computer Organization, Digital Logic, Microprocessors, Operating Systems.
Elective Courses: Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Data Mining, Computer Networks, Cyber Security, Database Systems.
Lab Courses: Programming Labs, Digital Logic Labs, Microprocessor Labs, Networking Labs.
Project Courses: Individual or group projects to design, develop, and test computer systems and applications.

Course Objectives

To provide students with a strong foundation in computer science and engineering principles.
To equip students with the skills and knowledge to design, develop, and test computer hardware and software systems.
To prepare students for careers in computer system engineering, software development, and related fields.
